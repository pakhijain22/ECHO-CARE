Red Flag Alerts

Recognize warning signs that require immediate medical attention

⚠️ Important Notice

These alerts are informational guidelines only and do NOT constitute medical diagnosis or advice.

EchoCare provides general safety information. Always trust your judgment and seek professional medical help when concerned about your loved one's health.

🚨 Call 112 or Emergency Services Immediately If:

🤕
Fall with Head Injury
  • Hit head during fall
  • Loss of consciousness (even briefly)
  • Bleeding from head or face
  • Confusion after hitting head
  • Severe headache
  • Vomiting after head injury
😵
Sudden Unconsciousness or Unresponsiveness
  • Cannot be awakened
  • Not responding to voice or touch
  • Passed out or fainted
  • Eyes closed and unresponsive
💔
Chest Pain or Heart Symptoms
  • Severe chest pain or pressure
  • Pain radiating to arm, jaw, or back
  • Shortness of breath with chest discomfort
  • Sudden irregular heartbeat
  • Profuse sweating with chest pain
🧠
Stroke Warning Signs (Act FAST)
  • Face drooping on one side
  • Arm weakness or numbness
  • Speech difficulty or slurred words
  • Time to call 112 immediately
  • Sudden vision loss in one or both eyes
  • Sudden severe headache
🫁
Severe Breathing Problems
  • Unable to breathe or gasping for air
  • Choking or can't speak
  • Blue lips or face
  • Extreme difficulty breathing
🩸
Severe Bleeding or Injury
  • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • Deep cuts or wounds
  • Broken bones visible through skin
  • Severe burns
🤢
Seizures
  • First-time seizure
  • Seizure lasting more than 5 minutes
  • Multiple seizures in a row
  • Difficulty breathing after seizure
  • Injury during seizure
🌀
Sudden Extreme Confusion or Delirium
  • Sudden dramatic change in mental state
  • Extreme agitation or combativeness
  • Hallucinations (seeing/hearing things)
  • Completely disoriented to time/place/person
  • Sudden inability to recognize family

⚠️ Contact Doctor Within 24 Hours If:

📉 Significant Behavioral Changes
  • Sudden increase in confusion frequency
  • New or worsening aggression
  • Refusing to eat or drink for more than 24 hours
  • Sudden sleep pattern changes
  • New incontinence issues
🤒 Physical Health Concerns
  • Fever over 100.4°F (38°C)
  • Persistent vomiting or diarrhea
  • Signs of dehydration
  • Persistent pain
  • Urinary problems or pain
💊 Medication Issues
  • Repeatedly missing medications
  • Possible medication side effects
  • Accidentally took wrong dose
  • New symptoms after starting medication
🚶 Falls Without Injury
  • Fall without serious injury but still concerning
  • Multiple falls in a short period
  • New balance or coordination problems
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
